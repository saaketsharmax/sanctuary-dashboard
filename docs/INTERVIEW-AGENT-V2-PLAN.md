## Part 3: Unique Questioning Techniques

### Technique 1: The Inversion Method
Instead of "Why will you succeed?", ask:
```
"If your startup fails in 18 months, what will be the reason?"
"What's the most likely way this doesn't work?"
"What would make you quit?"
```
**Why:** Forces intellectual honesty. Strong founders have thought about this deeply.

### Technique 2: The Time Machine Test
```
"It's 2029. You've won. Describe what the world looks like."
"It's 2029. You've failed. What happened?"
"Looking back from success, what was the turning point?"
```
**Why:** Reveals clarity of vision and self-awareness about risks.

### Technique 3: The User Story Immersion
```
"Pick one specific user. Give them a name. Walk me through their Tuesday."
"Show me the exact moment they feel the pain you're solving."
"What are they doing at 10:47am when they need you most?"
"What do they do immediately before and after using your product?"
```
**Why:** Tests whether founders truly understand their users at granular level.

### Technique 4: The Founder Chemistry Probe
```
"Tell me about your biggest disagreement with your co-founder. How did it resolve?"
"When was the last time you changed your mind because of something they said?"
"How do you make decisions when you disagree?"
"What does your co-founder think is YOUR biggest weakness?"
"If your co-founder quit tomorrow, what would you do?"
```
**Why:** Team breakdown is #1 startup killer. Surface the dynamics.

### Technique 5: The Second-Order Effects
```
"If you succeed, who loses?"
"What industry will you destroy? Who will fight back?"
"What's the unintended consequence of solving this problem?"
"What becomes possible that wasn't before?"
```
**Why:** Tests systems thinking and anticipation of resistance.

### Technique 6: The Evidence Tribunal
```
"You said users love it. Give me 3 specific quotes from the last week."
"You said it's painful. Quantify the pain in dollars or hours."
"You said you're growing. Show me the week-over-week numbers."
"You said they keep coming back. What's retention at day 7, 30, 90?"
```
**Why:** Separates narrative from data. Forces specificity.

### Technique 7: The Uncomfortable Truth
```
"What's the thing you're most afraid I'll ask about?"
"What do you tell investors that isn't quite true?"
"What would your harshest critic say about this idea?"
"What's the biggest lie you've told yourself about this startup?"
```
**Why:** Surfaces hidden concerns and tests integrity.

### Technique 8: The Insight Extraction
```
"What do you know about this problem that nobody else knows?"
"What's the secret that gives you an unfair advantage?"
"What did you learn from users that surprised you?"
"What's the non-obvious insight that makes this work?"
```
**Why:** Unique insights are the foundation of great startups.

### Technique 9: The Stress Test
```
"Your biggest competitor just raised $100M. What do you do?"
"Google launches a competing product tomorrow. Now what?"
"Your co-founder gets a $2M offer from Stripe. What happens?"
"You run out of money in 4 months. What's the plan?"
```
**Why:** Tests resilience and strategic thinking under pressure.

### Technique 10: The Love Test
```
"Who LOVES your product? Not likesâ€”LOVES."
"What would your most passionate user say about you?"
"If you shut down tomorrow, who would be devastated? How many?"
"Show me a message from a user that made you emotional."
```
**Why:** Passion predicts retention. Love signals are leading indicators.
